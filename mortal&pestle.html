ABOUT US

About Us

The roots of Mortar & Pestle stretch back over two thousand years, to the bustling markets of an ancient coastal city. In those days, herbalists and apothecaries were the keepers of wellness, carefully grinding fragrant spices, medicinal herbs, and rare minerals by hand. At the heart of every recipe was the same simple, enduring tool — the mortar and pestle.

As centuries passed, Amara’s descendants carried on the tradition, passing down secret spice blends and herbal recipes through generations. The family shop evolved — from a small stall in the market square to a cozy stone storefront lined with wooden shelves and earthen jars. Even as new tools and technologies came along, one thing never changed: everything was still hand-prepared with care, just as it had been in ancient times.

Today, Mortar & Pestle honors that legacy, blending the old world’s craftsmanship with the modern appetite for authentic, soulful flavors. Every jar, every cup, every blend is a connection to the past — a taste of history, reborn for today’s table.

At Mortar & Pestle, we believe every bite should be a little moment of joy. Nestled in the heart of Burlington, our bakery is where the comforting aroma of freshly baked bread, warm pastries, and decadent desserts greets you the moment you step inside.

We started with a simple dream: to bring people together over food that’s made with love, care, and the finest ingredients. From our buttery croissants to our rich chocolate cakes, every item is crafted fresh daily — no shortcuts, no compromises.

Our team of passionate bakers blends traditional recipes with creative flair, offering both timeless favorites and exciting seasonal specials. Whether you’re picking up your morning coffee and muffin, celebrating a birthday, or just treating yourself after a long day, we’ve got something to make it special.

At Mortar & Pestle, we’re more than a bakery — we’re your neighborhood gathering place, a sweet escape from the everyday. So, come in, share a smile, and let us make your day a little sweeter.

Our Team

At Mortar & Pestle, our team is more than a group of bakers — we’re a family united by a love of flavor, creativity, and community. From the early-morning dough mixers to the latte artists who perfect every pour, each member brings their own spark to the kitchen.

Our pastry chefs craft every recipe from scratch with fresh, locally sourced ingredients. Our baristas pair your favorite treats with perfectly brewed coffee and tea. And our front-of-house crew is here with a smile to make your visit feel like coming home.

Together, we’re not just serving pastries — we’re serving moments of joy, one bite at a time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Orders & Daily Specials

HOW DOES THE DAILY SPECIALS WORK?+
Each morning, our bakers craft a small-batch daily special — usually one or two items available in limited quantity. These are announced each day on our social channels by 7 AM. Once they're gone, they're gone! Daily specials rotate seasonally and are never repeated the same week.
CAN I RESERVE A DAILY SPECIAL SLICE IN ADVANCE?+
Yes! You can pre-order a daily special up to 24 hours in advance through our Order Online page or by calling us at (714) 555-0182. We hold pre-orders until closing time, so you're never left without your treat.
WHAT HAPPENS IF THE DAILY SPECIAL SELLS OUT BEFORE I ORDER?+
If a daily special sells out, we'll notify you immediately and offer a full refund or a swap for another item of equal value. We strongly recommend pre-ordering popular specials, especially on weekends.
CAN I ORDER MORE THAN ONE DAILY SPECIAL ITEM?+
To keep things fair for all customers, daily specials are limited to 2 per order. If you're planning a special event or larger gathering, please reach out to us in advance and we'll do our best to accommodate you.

Pickup & Store Info

WHERE DO I PICK UP MY ORDER?+
All orders are picked up at our bakery: 8899 Garden Grove Blvd, Garden Grove, CA 92844. When you arrive, simply give your name at the counter. Our team will have everything ready and boxed for you.
WHAT TIME SHOULD I COME TO PICK UP MY ORDER?+
You can pick up any time during our business hours (Mon–Sat 7 AM–7 PM, Sun 8 AM–5 PM). If you placed a timed order, please arrive within 30 minutes of your selected slot. Orders not picked up within 2 hours of the store closing will be held until the next business day.
DO YOU OFFER DELIVERY OR ONLY IN-STORE PICKUP?+
We offer both! In-store pickup is available during all business hours. Local delivery is available Tuesday through Sunday within a 10-mile radius of Garden Grove. Delivery orders over $35 ship free; otherwise a $4.99 delivery fee applies.
CAN SOMEONE ELSE PICK UP MY ORDER FOR ME?+
Absolutely! Just let them know the name the order was placed under. For orders paid with cash on pickup, the pickup person should have the order confirmation number handy.

Allergens & Dietary

DO YOU HAVE GLUTEN-FREE OR VEGAN OPTIONS?+
Yes! We offer a rotating selection of gluten-friendly and vegan items each week. Look for the 🌿 (vegan) and 🌾 (gluten-friendly) labels on our menu. Please note our kitchen handles wheat, dairy, eggs, and tree nuts, so cross-contact is possible.
ARE YOUR INGREDIENTS LOCALLY SOURCED?+
We partner with California farms for our eggs, butter, seasonal fruits, and fresh herbs. Our flour is stone-milled in small batches. We believe the quality of our ingredients is what makes every bite memorable.

The Secret to Our Perfectly Laminated Croissants

M

Chef Marie

Head Pastry Chef · 5 min read

After nearly a decade behind the bench, I can tell you with certainty: the croissant is the most humbling pastry in the French canon. It rewards patience and punishes shortcuts with ruthless precision. What follows are the three principles that finally — finally — transformed our morning croissants from merely good to something our regulars drive across Orange County for.

1. Cold Butter — Always

The single most common mistake home bakers and even trained pastry cooks make is working with butter that's too warm. Lamination is a mechanical process: you are physically encasing hundreds of thin sheets of fat between layers of dough. The moment your butter softens past 15°C, it stops behaving like a solid sheet and starts smearing into the dough — killing every layer you worked to create.

At Mortar & Pestle, we work in a kitchen chilled to 18°C on lamination days. We chill our rolling pins, our marble slabs, even our bench scrapers. The butter block — always a European-style 84% fat cultured butter — goes in cold, straight from the refrigerator, beaten with a rolling pin to pliability but never warmed by hand. If at any point you feel the butter threatening to break through the dough, stop. Wrap the whole thing and put it back in the fridge for 20 minutes. No exceptions.

"If your kitchen is warm, your croissants will tell you. They always do."

2. Patience Is a Technique

Every fold — called a tour in classic French pastry — must be followed by a full rest in the refrigerator. Not ten minutes. Not "until it feels cold." A full 30 minutes, minimum. We do three double turns, which gives us 64 layers of butter. Rush any of the three rests and the gluten tightens, the dough tears, and your layers collapse during baking.

I tell my apprentices to treat the resting stage like an ingredient. It's not waiting — it's working. The gluten is relaxing. The butter is re-solidifying. The structure you're building is setting itself. Respect the rest.

We also cold-proof our shaped croissants overnight — 8 to 12 hours in the refrigerator before baking. This slow fermentation develops flavor complexity that a quick room-temperature proof simply cannot replicate. You'll taste the difference immediately.

3. Never Rush the Fold

Rolling too hard, too fast, or too far is the most destructive thing you can do to a laminated dough. Roll with even, gentle pressure from the center outward. Stop before the edges — always leave a centimeter of unrolled dough on each end. And never use a sheeter at high speed for the final passes. The pressure tears the thin membrane of dough between your butter layers.

Watch the dough as you roll. If it begins to resist, it's telling you something. Relax it. If you see butter breaking through the surface in small patches, the fat is too warm — rest it. The dough communicates constantly; the baker's job is to listen.

When we cut our croissants, we use a razor-sharp blade in a single clean motion. Dragging the knife compresses the layers. A clean cut leaves them open, free to bloom in the oven into that unmistakable honeycomb interior.

A Note From Chef Marie

These aren't secrets so much as they are disciplines. Cold butter, patient rests, and a gentle hand — master these three and your croissants will speak for themselves. We'd love to see what you bake. Tag us at @mortarandpestlebakery and share your layers.

Your information helps us process orders, confirm pickups, respond to inquiries, and — if you opt in — share occasional updates and promotions. We never sell your data. We only share it with trusted service partners (such as payment processors or delivery services) when absolutely necessary.
